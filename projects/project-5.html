The Challenge

Climate data that causes anxiety instead of action

Climate change communication has a well-documented problem: the scale of the crisis is so vast that people either disengage (overwhelm) or overestimate their individual impact (guilt without guidance). Existing carbon calculators produced a number — but no meaningful next step.

Terra needed to be different: a platform that made environmental action feel personal, manageable, and — crucially — positive. Change through empowerment, not shame.

"How do we make climate action feel personal, not paralysing?"

01 — Research

Understanding why people don't act

I ran a mixed-methods research programme: a survey of 400 participants across age groups, a 3-week diary study with 15 participants tracking their environmental decision-making, and 20 in-depth interviews with both climate-engaged and climate-disengaged people.

The central finding was counterintuitive: the people most disengaged were not climate deniers — they were people who cared deeply but felt their actions were pointless compared to corporate and government inaction. The design problem was restoring agency, not building awareness.

😰 Paralysis by scale

73% of climate-aware participants felt personal actions were "meaningless" against systemic issues. This belief was the primary barrier to any action at all.

🔢 Number blindness

"I've avoided carbon calculators because they just make me feel terrible and I don't know what to do next." — 11 of 20 interview participants expressed this sentiment.

🤝 Social proof matters

When participants learned that someone like them had made a specific change, the likelihood of adoption increased by 3–4× compared to reading about abstract impact statistics.

02 — Design Framework

Three principles that guided everything

🎯
Personal, not global

Every metric is contextualised to the user's life. "Your travel footprint is 2× higher than similar people in your city" hits harder than abstract CO₂ numbers.

✅
Actions over guilt

Every piece of data is paired with 1–3 specific, achievable actions. The tone is always empowering, never accusatory.

📈
Visible progress

Users can see their footprint reduce over time, creating a positive feedback loop. Momentum is more motivating than perfection.

03 — The Dashboard

Data as a tool, not a verdict

The dashboard was the hardest problem to solve. Early versions showed total carbon numbers prominently — this tested poorly. Users immediately felt judged. The solution: lead with trend and momentum, not absolute numbers. The most important thing isn't where you are — it's which direction you're moving.
